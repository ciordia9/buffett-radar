## Major News Event: Kraft Heinz Exit Signal

### Story: Greg Abel's First Portfolio Cleanup Move

**Source:** CNBC (Alex Crippen), January 24, 2026

**Key Development:** Just three weeks into his tenure as CEO, Greg Abel has signaled Berkshire may exit its troubled Kraft Heinz (KHC) stake. An SEC filing on Tuesday January 20 revealed Berkshire may sell "up to" almost all of its 27.5% stake, currently worth $7.5 billion.

**Historical Context:**
- Berkshire joined with 3G Capital to buy H.J. Heinz for $23.3B in 2013
- Kraft merged with Heinz in 2015 (deal championed by Buffett)
- Peak value: ~$30B in 2016
- Current value: $7.5B (down 75% from peak)
- **Total loss:** ~$2.3B (paid $9.8B, now worth $7.5B)
- Buffett admitted in 2019: "We overpaid for Kraft"

**Market Reaction:**
- KHC fell 7% immediately after filing, to $21.99 (near 6-year low)
- Ended week at $23.20 (-1.4% for the week)

**Analyst Interpretation:**
- **Morningstar's Greggory Warren:** "We view the timing of this sale as being reflective of Abel's desire to clean up and pare down the investment portfolio early in his tenure."
- Represents Abel's willingness to exit Buffett-era mistakes rather than hold indefinitely
- Signals proactive portfolio management approach

**Implications:**
1. **Capital deployment pattern emerging:** Abel is cleaning up legacy positions (Kraft Heinz) while deploying into wholly-owned industrial/utility assets (OxyChem $9.7B, BHE transmission $1.2B)
2. **Cash position pressure:** Exiting KHC adds $7.5B to already massive cash pile (~$372B after OxyChem), increasing pressure to deploy
3. **Philosophical shift:** Abel appears more willing than Buffett to admit mistakes and move on quickly
